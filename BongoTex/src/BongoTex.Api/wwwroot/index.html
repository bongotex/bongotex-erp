Production pipeline (cutting → sewing → finishing)

Track cutting (fabric kg, price per kg, fabric cost, pieces cut per day), sewing (pieces per day), and finishing (pieces finished per day). Use the same cutting number at cut, sew, and finish for one bundle — you can type a short code (e.g. 1); the system adds the row date’s calendar year if you omit YYYY- (e.g. 2026-1). That number must be unique per factory on cutting (one cutting line per number; edit quantities instead of duplicating). At finishing, use two fields: cutting number (same pipeline bundle as cut/sew) and item number (same layout as Add Inventory Item: prefix dropdown + SKU field; the next free SKU loads when you change prefix, like inventory) — saving sends the full inventory SKU and links that item to open cut/sew rows for the same cutting number. This is separate from Post Production stock below unless you align quantities yourself. The daily WIP report shows per UTC day: activity that day and cumulative WIP (unsewing = all cut − all sewn; unfinished = all sewn − all finished).

Add Expense

After changing category or scope, use the green Add Expense button below the fields. Hard-refresh the page (Ctrl+F5) if the button looks grey or does nothing. Scope sets garment factory, print factory, sales centre, or owner payout — no separate department field. Expenses are blocked if they would exceed factory petty, manager cash, or the selected sales centre drawer balance (same as cash transfers).

Party and Description may be Bangla or English. Amount must use English digits (0–9): click Amount, press Win+Space until the taskbar shows ENG, then type e.g. 5000.

Garment and print factory operating expenses are paid from factory petty cash (same physical cash box). On hand is never below 0 — if spend was posted without a transfer, you will see an unrecorded inflow warning. Transfer Manager → Factory petty before paying supplier/salary/rent/daily expenses.

Cash pool transfers

Record physical cash between sales center drawers, the manager, and factory petty. You cannot transfer more than the available balance shown for the From pool. Sales centers send cash to the Manager only (not directly to factory petty). Only when From or To is Sales center do you pick which shop — e.g. Manager → Factory petty needs no sales center (manager hands cash straight to the factory safe). Separate from Add Expense for P/L. Tip: for the same physical center→manager handoff, use either a Manager remittance expense or a Sales center → Manager transfer — not both.

Factory attendance sheet

Click a day cell to cycle: blank → P (present, green) → A (absent, red) → blank. Only P days count in payroll.

All reports

Finance summaries, ledgers, and history. Use for expenses and cash transfers, for salary processing, and for supplier purchases.

Daily factory P/L

Finishing pcs × current registered production cost (by SKU prefix on Registration) + scrap cash − rent share − salary share − daily expenses. Rent and salary: monthly ÷ working days (Fridays = 0).

Daily sales centre P/L

Net sales − product cost COGS − rent share − salary share − daily expenses. Rent and salary: monthly ÷ working days (Fridays = 0).

Sales center report (date to date)

Per sales center for the UTC date range: stock received from factory (pcs and value), current stock on hand (pcs and value), pieces sold, sales amount, initial payment at sale, cash collected on due, credit sales, remaining due on those sales, returns, sale discount vs settlement discount, remittance to manager, and sales-center expenses (daily, salary, rent, other). Stock on hand is as of now, not limited to the date range.
